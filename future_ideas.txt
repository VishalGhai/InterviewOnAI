InterviewOnAI — Future Feature Ideas (History Log)
Purpose: track scaling/growth feature ideas so they are not

DATE: 2026-06-26
----------------------------------------------------------------

IDEA 1 — Voice-Based Mock Interviews (Speech In/Out)
Today the interview is fully text-driven, which does not reflect
how real interviews feel. Add a voice mode where the AI asks each
question aloud via text-to-speech and the candidate answers by
speaking, with browser speech-to-text transcribing the response
before Gemini evaluates it. This trains the two things text mode
cannot: verbal fluency and thinking on your feet under time
pressure. Layer in spoken-delivery metrics alongside the existing
five — speaking pace, filler-word count ("um", "like"), pause
length, and response latency — so the report card grades how a
candidate sounds, not just what they say. A "phone-screen
simulation" preset would mimic a recruiter call. This feature is a
strong differentiator versus static question banks, justifies a
premium voice tier, and dramatically increases session length and
perceived realism. It reuses the current question/evaluation
pipeline, so most work is front-end audio capture plus a few new
scoring prompts. Accessibility also improves for users who prefer
talking over typing, widening the addressable market.

IDEA 2 — Company-Specific & Role-Specific Interview Packs
The platform currently offers 20 generic topics or a pasted job
description. Introduce curated, branded interview packs targeting
the companies and roles candidates actually want — "Amazon SDE-2",
"Google L4 System Design", "Startup Full-Stack", "Data Engineer".
Each pack tunes Gemini's prompts toward that company's known
interview style (e.g., Amazon Leadership Principles + behavioral
STAR scoring, FAANG-grade system design rubrics) and difficulty
mix. This is a powerful acquisition hook because it maps directly
to high-intent search terms ("Amazon interview practice") and gives
users a concrete reason to pay per pack rather than per question
count. Packs can be sold individually or bundled into a
subscription, opening recurring revenue beyond the current
pay-per-session model. Internally, packs are mostly prompt
templates plus metadata, so the engineering cost is low while the
marketing and SEO upside is high. Over time, usage data reveals
which packs convert best, guiding which to expand. This also sets
up a natural content-marketing engine: one landing page per
company/role pack to capture organic traffic.

IDEA 3 — Referral & Shareable Achievement System
Growth currently relies on direct traffic; there is no viral loop.
Add a referral program where each user gets a unique invite link
that grants both the referrer and the new signup bonus free
questions or a discounted pack. Pair this with shareable, visually
polished achievement cards generated at the end of strong sessions
("Scored A on System Design — Hard", with the five-dimension
radar) that users can post to LinkedIn, X, or WhatsApp. Because the
audience is job seekers actively signaling skills, social sharing
is naturally on-brand and free advertising. The existing report
card and metrics make this nearly free to build — render the score
breakdown to an image and attach the referral link. Track referral
attribution in the payments/profiles tables to measure CAC impact.
Milestone streaks (e.g., "7-day practice streak") add a retention
hook and another shareable moment. This converts satisfied users
into a distribution channel, lowering customer acquisition cost
exactly where a bootstrapped product needs leverage most, and
compounds with the company-pack landing pages for SEO + social
reach.

IDEA 4 — B2B / Education Tier for Colleges & Bootcamps
Selling one session at a time caps revenue at the individual level.
Introduce an organization tier where colleges, coding bootcamps,
and placement cells buy seats in bulk for their students. This
needs an admin dashboard showing cohort-level analytics — average
scores by topic, weakest skill areas across the group, completion
rates, and per-student progress over time — built on the data
already captured in the interviews and questions tables. Add
instructor-assigned practice sets and exportable reports for
placement coordinators. B2B contracts deliver predictable,
higher-value recurring revenue and a much shorter sales cycle than
acquiring thousands of individual users one ₹49 session at a time.
India's large ecosystem of engineering colleges and upskilling
platforms is an ideal initial market that matches the current INR
pricing and audience. Technically this is mostly an
authorization/role layer (org admin vs. student), aggregate
analytics queries, and seat management on top of Supabase. It also
generates testimonials and case studies that strengthen the
consumer funnel. This is likely the single highest-leverage move
for scaling revenue beyond linear per-session growth.

IDEA 5 — Adaptive Learning Path with Weak-Area Drilling
Right now every session is a one-off; the rich per-dimension scores
stored per question are not fed back into future sessions. Build an
adaptive learning engine that analyzes a user's history to identify
persistent weak spots (e.g., consistently low "Depth" on System
Design) and automatically recommends targeted follow-up drills.
Present a personalized dashboard: a skill-mastery map across topics
and the five dimensions, trend lines showing improvement over time,
and a "Recommended Next Session" that biases question selection
toward weaknesses using spaced-repetition timing so concepts
resurface before they fade. This transforms the product from a
practice tool into a structured prep program, the core driver of
retention and repeat purchases — users return because the platform
remembers them and visibly moves them forward. Attach short,
AI-generated micro-explanations or curated resource links for each
weak area so practice converts into learning. All of this builds on
data already in the schema, so the value is largely unlocked
through analytics and smarter prompt selection rather than new
infrastructure. It directly increases lifetime value and justifies
a recurring subscription over one-time payments.

DATE: 2026-06-27

IDEA 1 — Resume-Driven Interviews + ATS/Resume Optimizer
Today candidates start from a pasted job description or a topic
button, but the single most personal input — their own resume — is
ignored. Let users upload a resume (PDF/DOCX) and have Gemini parse
it to (a) generate interview questions grounded in their actual
projects, stack, and claimed accomplishments ("You listed Kafka on
Project X — walk me through the partitioning strategy"), and (b)
return a separate resume critique: ATS keyword gaps versus a target
JD, weak bullet points rewritten in impact-metric form, and
red-flag claims likely to trigger probing. This turns the platform
into an end-to-end prep funnel — fix the resume, then rehearse
defending it — and creates a strong top-of-funnel acquisition hook
since "free resume review" is a high-intent search magnet. It also
exposes natural upsell: a paid "resume + 3 tailored sessions"
bundle. Technically it reuses the existing question/evaluation
pipeline; the new work is file parsing plus two prompt templates,
and parsed resume data can seed the adaptive engine and company
packs proposed earlier. The personalization sharply increases
perceived realism and session relevance, which lifts conversion
from free trial to paid.

IDEA 2 — Behavioral & HR Round Mode with STAR Scoring
The product is purely technical, yet most candidates are rejected
in behavioral and HR rounds, not on algorithms. Add a dedicated
behavioral mode that asks "Tell me about a time…" and situational
questions (conflict, failure, leadership, ambiguity) and scores
answers against the STAR framework — Situation, Task, Action,
Result — instead of the five technical dimensions. The report card
would flag missing structure ("no measurable Result"), rambling, or
answers that never reach the candidate's own contribution. Add
company-culture variants (Amazon Leadership Principles, "why this
company", salary-negotiation role-play) and a "full loop" preset
mixing technical and behavioral rounds back-to-back to mirror a
real onsite. This roughly doubles the addressable use cases per
user and appeals to non-engineering roles (product, sales, ops,
management) — meaningfully widening the market beyond developers.
It is largely a new set of evaluation prompts and a rubric toggle
layered on the existing chat/report infrastructure, so engineering
cost is modest. Behavioral prep is also where candidates feel least
confident, making it a compelling reason to buy and to return for
repeated practice before a specific interview date.

IDEA 3 — Live Coding Challenge Mode with In-Browser Editor
Technical interviews increasingly center on writing and running
real code, but the platform only accepts typed prose answers. Add
a coding mode with an embedded editor (Monaco/CodeMirror) plus
syntax highlighting and a sandboxed run-and-test harness so
candidates solve DSA and practical problems by actually coding.
Gemini then evaluates correctness against hidden test cases,
time/space complexity, edge-case handling, code readability, and
the candidate's verbal reasoning — and can inject realistic
follow-ups ("now make it O(n)"). A countdown timer recreates
interview pressure, and a replay of approach helps users review
how they got stuck. This closes the biggest credibility gap versus
competitors like LeetCode and HackerRank while keeping
InterviewOnAI's differentiator: AI feedback on the thought process,
not just pass/fail. It justifies a premium tier, dramatically
increases session length and engagement, and produces rich
performance data for the adaptive engine. The main build is editor
integration and a secure execution sandbox (a hosted runner or
WASM-based execution), which is the heaviest lift here but the
clearest path to being taken seriously by serious engineering
candidates.

IDEA 4 — Adaptive Follow-Up Questioning with Interviewer Personas
Real interviewers probe; this platform asks a fixed question, scores
it, and moves on. Make the AI conversational within each question:
when an answer is shallow, vague, or wrong, Gemini asks a targeted
follow-up ("Why would that approach fail at scale?") before
advancing, exactly as a human interviewer drills down. Layer on
selectable interviewer personas — Friendly Mentor, Time-Pressured
Recruiter, Skeptical Senior Engineer, Stress-Test Panel — each
shaping tone, follow-up aggressiveness, and patience. This makes
sessions feel like genuine dialogue rather than a graded quiz,
sharply raising realism and the sense that the candidate is being
"interviewed" rather than tested. It also surfaces depth far better
than a single-shot answer, improving the accuracy of the five-
dimension scores. Engineering-wise it is a control-flow change in
the chat loop (allow N follow-ups per question, gated by the
answer's score) plus persona system-prompts — no new
infrastructure. Personas create variety that drives repeat sessions
("try the stress panel") and map cleanly onto premium tiers.
Capping follow-ups keeps cost and length bounded. This is one of
the cheapest features to ship that most increases perceived product
quality and word-of-mouth.

IDEA 5 — Multilingual & Regional-Language Support (India-First)
Pricing in ₹ signals an India-first audience, yet the entire
experience is English-only. Many strong candidates — especially
from tier-2/3 cities and non-CS backgrounds — think and articulate
more confidently in Hindi or regional languages, and a growing
share of roles (support, sales, field, regional product) are
interviewed partly in the local language. Let users choose the
interview language so Gemini asks questions, accepts answers, and
returns feedback in Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, and others, with
a "bilingual" option that mirrors real Indian interviews mixing
English technical terms with a local conversational layer. This
unlocks a large, under-served segment that current English-only
competitors ignore, strengthening the INR positioning and opening
partnerships with regional colleges and upskilling platforms.
Gemini already handles these languages natively, so the work is
mostly UI localization, a language selector, and prompt
instructions specifying the response language — low engineering
cost for a large market expansion. It also improves accessibility
and inclusivity, generates region-specific landing pages for SEO,
and pairs naturally with the behavioral and B2B/education
directions to capture the domestic placement-prep market at scale.

IDEA 1 — Video Interview Mode with Presence & Body-Language Analysis
Prior ideas covered voice (audio) practice, but real onsite and
video-call interviews are judged as much on presence as on words.
Add an opt-in webcam mode that records the candidate during answers
and analyzes non-verbal delivery: eye-contact ratio (looking at
camera vs. away), posture and fidgeting, smile/engagement, and
on-screen framing/lighting quality. Combine this with the existing
five content dimensions to produce a "presence score" and concrete
coaching ("you looked away 40% of the time", "lean in, sit centered").
A "video-call simulation" preset mirrors a remote first-round with a
picture-in-picture self-view so candidates rehearse exactly what a
Zoom/Meet panel sees. This is a sharp differentiator versus text- and
even audio-only competitors and justifies a top premium tier. Privacy
must be explicit: processing happens client-side or is opt-in and never
stored without consent, which is also a marketing trust signal. Most
analysis can run in-browser via lightweight face/pose models
(MediaPipe/TensorFlow.js), keeping API cost near zero, while Gemini
synthesizes the coaching narrative. It dramatically raises realism,
session length, and perceived value for candidates preparing for
remote-first hiring pipelines.

IDEA 2 — Public Question Library + Programmatic SEO Content Engine
Growth today depends on people already knowing the site. Turn the
platform's core asset — AI-generated questions with model answers and
five-dimension rubrics — into a large, publicly browsable, search-
indexable library (e.g. "Top 50 React Hooks Interview Questions with
Answers"). Each topic and difficulty gets its own crawlable landing
page with high-quality sample questions, a model answer, common
mistakes, and a prominent "Practice this live with AI feedback" CTA
that funnels visitors into a paid session. This creates a compounding,
near-free organic acquisition channel targeting the exact high-intent
searches job seekers already make, and it pairs naturally with the
company/role packs proposed earlier (one page per pack). Because the
content is generated once and cached statically, it fits the current
GitHub-Pages/static architecture with minimal runtime cost — pre-render
pages, add structured-data (FAQ schema) markup, sitemaps, and internal
links. Freemium logic: reading is free, but the interactive AI
evaluation, follow-ups, and report card stay paid. Over time,
aggregate answer data reveals which questions convert best, guiding
which pages to expand. This is the lowest-cost, highest-leverage
top-of-funnel growth engine for a bootstrapped product.

IDEA 3 — "Practice This Job" Browser Extension & Job-Board Integration
Candidates find roles on LinkedIn, Naukri, Indeed, and company career
pages — not on InterviewOnAI. Ship a lightweight browser extension that
injects a "Practice for this role" button directly onto job listings;
one click scrapes the visible job description and deep-links into the
platform's existing JD mode with the interview pre-configured. This
removes the biggest friction point (copy-pasting a JD) and meets users
at the exact moment of highest intent — right after they see a role
they want. It is a strong distribution and virality lever: the
extension itself is discoverable in the Chrome/Edge stores under
"interview practice", and every use reinforces the habit of preparing
before applying. Technically it reuses the current JD pipeline
untouched; the new work is a small content-script that reads the page
and opens a pre-filled session URL, plus auth hand-off. It also unlocks
future contextual features (salary-range hints, company-pack matching).
For an India-first audience, first-class Naukri/Instahyre support is a
differentiator global competitors ignore. Low build cost, a new
acquisition surface outside the website, and a durable retention hook
that ties the product to the real job-search workflow.

IDEA 4 — Verified Skill Certification, Public Profiles & Leaderboards
Scores today live privately inside a session and vanish. Turn strong
performances into a durable, shareable credential: after a rigorous
proctored assessment (fixed question count, Hard difficulty, timed,
no retries), issue a verifiable "InterviewOnAI Certified — System
Design, A-grade" badge with a public verification URL candidates add to
LinkedIn and resumes. Layer a public opt-in profile showing verified
skill badges and trend lines, plus topic leaderboards ("Top 100 in
Python this month") for competitive motivation. This creates two
scaling engines at once: every shared certificate and profile is free,
credible advertising to the exact audience (recruiters and peers), and
credentialing is a monetizable event separate from practice (a paid
"take the certified assessment" flow). It leans entirely on data and
scoring the platform already produces — the build is mainly a
tamper-evident verification page, badge rendering, and aggregate
ranking queries on the existing interviews table. Guardrails matter:
certified runs must be harder and non-repeatable to keep the badge
meaningful, and any employer-facing claim should carry a "practice
assessment" disclaimer. Over time, recruiter interest in verified
profiles opens a B2B talent-signal channel alongside the education tier.

IDEA 5 — Interview Countdown Planner with Smart Reminders & Re-Engagement
Most users cram right before a specific interview date, then disappear.
Capture that date: let a user enter "My interview is on July 14 for a
Backend role at Company X", and the platform auto-generates a
personalized day-by-day prep plan — which topics and difficulties to
practice each day, a realistic session cadence, and a final "day-before
confidence run". Pair it with opt-in email/push (Web Push/PWA) reminders
that nudge users back for each planned session and re-engage lapsed
ones ("2 days left — you haven't practiced System Design yet"). This
directly attacks the product's biggest leak: one-and-done usage. A
date-anchored plan converts a single purchase into a multi-day habit,
raising sessions-per-user, completion rates, and repeat revenue, and
the reminder channel becomes an owned re-activation asset independent of
paid ads. It builds on data already captured and complements the
adaptive weak-area engine by sequencing what to practice and when.
Technically it is a plan-generation prompt, a small schedule table, and
a notification worker (Supabase scheduled function + Web Push) — modest
cost. It also creates natural urgency-based upsell moments as the
interview date approaches.
